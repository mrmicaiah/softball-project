Softball's First Name Was "Kitten Ball"

When softball was invented in 1887, it wasn't even called softball! It went by Kitten Ball, "Mush Ball," and "Diamond Ball" before the official name "softball" was chosen in 1926.

2
where it started

Born Indoors in Chicago

The very first softball game was played indoors in Chicago. The "ball" was a boxing glove tied with string, and the "bat" was a broom handle. Fancy, right?

The Famous Windmill Pitch

That cool full-circle pitch where the pitcher's arm whips all the way around? It's called the windmill. It's unique to fastpitch softball — you won't see it in baseball!

5

Less Time to Swing Than MLB

Top softball pitchers throw around 70 mph — but the mound is way closer than in baseball. So softball batters actually have less reaction time than MLB hitters facing 100 mph fastballs!
